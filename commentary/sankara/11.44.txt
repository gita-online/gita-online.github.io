<i>Tasmat</i>, therefore; <i>pranamya</i>, by bowing down; and <i>pranidhaya kayam</i>, 
prostrating, laying, the body completely down; <i>prasadaye</i>, I seek to propitiate; 
<i>tvam</i>, You; who are <i>isam</i>, God, the Lord; and are <i>idyam</i>, adorable. 
Deva, O God; You are Your part, <i>arhasi</i>, should; <i>sodhum</i>, bear with, i.e. 
forgive (my faults); <i>iva</i>, as would; a <i>pita</i>, father; forgive all the 
faults <i>putrasya</i>, of a son; and as a <i>sakha</i>, friend; the faults <i>sakhyuh</i>, 
of a friend; or as a <i>priyah</i>, lover; forgives the faults <i>priyayah</i>, of a beloved.
