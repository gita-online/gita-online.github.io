The first line of the verse beginning with 'benefactor,' etc. is a single compound word. <i>Visisyate</i>, he excels, i.e. he is the best among all those who are established in Yoga-(a different reading is <i>vimucyate</i>, he becomes free); <i>sama buddhih</i>, who has sameness of view, i.e. whose mind is not engaged with the question of who one is and what he does; with regard to a <i>suhrd</i>, benefactor - one who does some good without consideration of return; <i>mitram</i>, a friend, one who is affectionate; <i>arih</i>, a foe; <i>udasinah</i>, a neutral, who sides with nobody; <i>madhyasthah</i>, an arbiter, who is a well-wisher of two conflicting parties; <i>dvesyah</i>, the hateful, who is repulsive to oneself; <i>bandhuh</i>, a relative;- to all these as also <i>sadhusu</i>, with regard to good people, who follow the scriptures; <i>api ca</i>, and even; <i>papesu</i>, sinners, who perform prohibited actions-with regard to all of them.
