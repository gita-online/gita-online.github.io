<i>Yatinam</i>, to the monks; <i>yata-cetasam</i>, who have control over their internal organ; <i>kama krodha-viyuktanam</i>, who 
are free from desire and anger; <i>vidita-atmanam</i>, who have known the Self, i.e. who have full realization; <i>vartate</i>, 
there is; <i>brahma-nir-vanam</i>, absorption in Brahman, Liberation; <i>abhitah</i>, either way, whether living or dead.<br><br>

Immediate Liberation of the monks who are steadfast in full realization has been stated. And the Lord has said, and will say, 
at every stage that Karma-yoga, undertaken as a dedication to Brahman, to God, by surrendering all activities to God, 
leads to Liberation through the stages of purification of the heart, attainment of Knowledge, and renunciation of all actions.
