<i>Yatha</i>, as; <i>ekam</i>, the one; <i>ravih</i>, sun; <i>prakasayati</i>, illumines; <i>imam</i>, this; <i>krtsnam</i>, whole; <i>lokam</i>, 
world tatha, similarly; Who? <i>ksetri</i>, the Knower of the field, i.e. the supreme Self, though one; <i>prakasayati</i>, illumines; <i>krtsnam</i>, 
the whole; <i>ksetram</i>, field, from the 'great elements' to 'fortitude'. Here the illustration of the sun serves to highlight two 
aspects of the Self, viz that, like the sun, the Self is one in all the fields, and that It remains unaffected. The next verse is meant for 
summarizing the idea of the whole of this chapter.
