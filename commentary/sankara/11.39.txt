<p>
    You are <i>vayuh</i>, Air; <i>yamah</i>, Death; and <i>agnih</i>, Fire; 
    <i>varunah</i>, the god of the waters; <i>sasankah</i>, the moon; 
    <i>prajapatih</i>, the Lord of the creatures - Kasyapa and others; and 
    <i>pra-pitamahah</i>, the Great-grandfather, i.e. the Father ever of Brahma 
    (Hiranyagarbha). <i>Namo</i>, salutations; <i>namah</i>, salutation; 
    <i>astu</i>, be; <i>te</i>, to You; <i>sahasra-krtvah</i>, a thousand times. 
    <i>Punah ca bhuyah api namo te</i>, salutation to You again and again; 
    <i>namah</i>, salutation!
</p> 

<p>
    The suffix krtvasuc (after sahasra) indicates performance and repetition of 
    the act of salutation a number of times. The words <i>punah ca bhuyah api</i> 
    (again and again) indicate his own dissatisfaction owing to abundance of 
    reverence and devotion.
</p>
