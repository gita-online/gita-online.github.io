<i>Rajan</i>, O King, Dhrtarastra; <i>uktva</i>, having spoken evam, thus, in the manner stated above; <i>tatah</i>, 
thereafter; <i>harih</i>, Hari, Narayana; <i>maha yogeswarah</i>, the great Master of Yoga - who is great 
(mahan) and also the master (isvara) of Yoga; <i>darasyamasa</i>, showed; <i>parthaya</i>, to the son of Prtha; 
the paramam, supreme; <i>aisvaram</i>, divine; <i>rupam</i>, form, the Cosmic form.
