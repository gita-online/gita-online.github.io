<i>Prabhuh</i>, the Self; <i>na srjati</i>, does not create; <i>lokasya</i>, for anyone; 
<i>kartrtvam</i>, agentship, by saying 'Do this'; or even karmani, any objects - 
such objects as chariot, pot, palace, etc. which are intensely longed for; nor even 
<i>karma-phala samyogam</i>, association with the results of actions association of 
the creator of a chariot etc. with the result of his work.<br><br>

<b>Objection</b>: If the embodied one does not do anything himself, and does not 
make others do, then who is it that engages in work by doing and making others do? 

<b>Answer</b>: <i>Tu</i>, but; it is <i>svabhavah</i>, Nature - one's own (sva) 
nature (bhava) - characterized as ignorance, Maya, which will be spoken of in, 
'Since this divine Maya' (7.14); <i>pravartate</i>, that acts.
