<i>Parama-atma</i>, the supreme Self; <i>jita-atmanah</i>, of one who has control over the aggregate of his body and organs; <i>prasantasya</i>, who is tranquil, who is a monk with his internal organ placid; <i>samahitah</i>, becomes manifest, i.e. becomes directly manifest as his own Self.<br><br>

Moreover, (he should be equipoised) <i>sita-usna-sukha-duhkhesu</i>, in the midst of cold and heat, happiness and sorrow; <i>tatha</i>, as also; <i>mana apamanayoh</i> in honour and dishonour, adoration and despise.
