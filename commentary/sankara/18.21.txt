<i>Tu</i>, but; <i>viddhi</i>, know; <i>tat</i>, that; jnanam, knowledge; to be <i>rajasam</i>, originating from 
rajas; <i>yat</i>, which; <i>sarvesu bhutesu</i>, amidst all things; <i>vetti</i>, apprehends-since knowledge 
cannot be an agent of hends-since knowledge cannot be an agent of action, therefore the meaning implied is, 
'that, knowledge...through which one apprehends...'; <i>nana-bhavan</i>, the different entities; 
<i>prthagvidhan</i>, of various kinds, i.e., those possessing diverse characteristics and different from 
oneself; prthakrvena, as distinct, as separate in each body.
