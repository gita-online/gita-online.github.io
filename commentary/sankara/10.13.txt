<p>
    <i>Bhavan</i>, You; are the param brahma, supreme Brahman, the supreme Self; the 
    <i>param dhama</i>, supreme Light; the <i>paramam pavitram</i>, supreme Sanctifier. 
    <i>Sarve</i>, all; <i>rsayah</i>, the sages Vasistha and others; tatha, as also; the 
    <i>devarisih</i>, divine sage; <i>naradah</i>, Narada; Asita and Devala <i>ahuh</i>, 
    call; <i>tvam</i>, You; thus: <i>Sasvatam</i>, the eternal; <i>divyam</i>, divine; 
    <i>purusam</i>, Person; <i>adi-devam</i>, the Primal God, the God who preceded all the 
    gods; <i>ajam</i>, the birthless; <i>vibhum</i>, the Omnipresent capable of assuming 
    diverse forms. 
</p>

<p>
    And even Vyasa also speaks in this very way. <i>Ca</i>, and; <i>svayam</i>, You Yourself; 
    <i>eva</i>, verily; <i>bravisi</i>, tell; <i>me</i>, me (so).
</p>
