<i>Ahankaram</i>, egotism-that which considers the Self to which have been imputed actual and imaginary qualities as 'I am this', which is called ignorance and is most 
painful, and is the source of all ills as also of all evil deeds; so also <i>balam</i>, power, which seeds to defear others and is associated with passion and desire; 
<i>darpam</i>, arrogance, a particular defect abiding in the mind, on the upsurge of which one transgresses righteousness; <i>kamam</i>, passion with regard to women 
and others; <i>krodham</i>, anger at things tha are undesirable; <i>samsritah</i>, resorting to these and other great evils; and further, <i>pradvisantah</i>, hating; 
<i>mam</i>, Me, God-transgression of My commands is hatred (towards Me); indulging in that, <i>atma-para dehesu</i>, in their own and others' bodies as the witness 
of their intellects and actions; (they become) <i>abhyasuyakah</i>, envious by nature, intolerant of the qualities of those who tread the right path.
