<p><div align=justify><p>
<i>1) pravrttinca</i> is actions performed  under  the  auspices  of  <i>dharma</i>  or
righteousness
<i>2) nivrittinca</i> is refraining from actions opposed to <i>dharma</i>  which  gives
liberation
<i>3) karya-akarye</i> is knowing what is appropriate according to  time,  place
and circumstance whether adhering to <i>pravrittinca</i> or <i>nivrittinca</i>
<i>4) bhaya-abhaye</i> is fear stemming from not adhering to the injunctions  of
the Vedic scriptures and fearlessness is by observing  the  ordinances
of the Vedic scriptures
<i>5) bandha</i> is <i>samsara</i> or enslavement in the perpetual cycle of  birth  and
death
<i>6) moksa</i> is liberation from material existence and release from <i>samsara</i>
<p>
Lord Krishna is affirming the essentials of those whose <i>buddhi</i> or  intellect
is situated in <i>sattva guna</i> the mode  of  goodness  as  only  they  have  the
ability to realise these points.
